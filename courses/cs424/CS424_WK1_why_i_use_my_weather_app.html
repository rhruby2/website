To prepare for weather is to be prepared

The Weather application I use is from the omnipotent conglomerate Google, simply named Google Weather, I think. This has proven to be more helpful and efficient comparing to the default weather application provided by my android smartphone which uses Accuweather. With Google weather, I specifically like the ability to view days in an hourly spread, which lets me prepare for each part of the day in relation to my schedule. This can include preparation for 10 degree rises or drops easily visible by a temperature graph, or minutes of being outside in pouring rain that may not have shown up on the general daily weather forecast. All data is presented in-app, such that it does not redirect you to a website, like the other said application. I also appreciate the 10-day spread of weather, for weather preparation in the future, for example, if its time to buy some warmer gloves or not. Finally, all data is easily readable through simple various and related icons with a non-compact design.
